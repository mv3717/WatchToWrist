1950’s Gruen Precision

£250


Classic aesthetics, well-executed details and a unique engraving from 1955 to commemorate 10 years of employment


About the Watch

This Gruen watch is a perfect example of how beauty can derive from simplicity, through well thought out and executed details: the cream dial, distinctive hour and minute hands, applied hour markers, multi-facetted lugs and the large, chunky crown all come together to create a design which is both classic and distinctive.

Flip the watch over and you are met with a personalised engraving which bears testament to its previous life. The engraving reads “A.A.C.C. 10 YEARS 1-18-55 WILLIAM McMAHAN”. Personalised engravings were commonplace during the 20th century to commemorate the special occasion on which a watch was gifted, such as a graduation or a birthday. The engraving on this specific watch reveals that it was gifted on 18th January 1955 to celebrate 10 years of employment. The thought that 64 years ago this watch was gifted to celebrate this very special occassion is a testament to how vintage watches can connect us to the past in a very personal way.

This watch further sets itself apart thanks to the “Precision” designation which can be read on the dial and on the Gruen N710 SS automatic movement. This reveals that the movement came from Gruen’s Precision Factory, located in Switzerland, where the company designed and manufactured many of their own movements before they were sent to the United States to be assembled into American made cases. The original Precision Factory is now owned by none other than Rolex and is apparently used as an administration facility.

It also happens that the first watch ever worn by James Bond on screen, in Dr. No (1963), was a Gruen Precision. Yes, really. And he did so while in a tuxedo at a casino table. It doesn’t really get any cooler than that.



About the Brand

The Gruen Watch Company was formerly one of the largest watch manufacturers in the United States. It was in business from about 1894 to 1958 and was based in Cincinnati, Ohio. Gruen was one of the first US watch companies to offer movements produced in Switzerland but adjusted, dialled and cased in the United States. The first Gruen wristwatches were introduced in 1908. Gruen was one of a very few companies to take wristwatches seriously this early, when pocket watches were the norm, seeing their potential in spite of disappointing early sales to male customers.

From manufacturing the gauges for metal detectors used after the Pearl Harbor attack to designing pilot's watches for Pan American World Airways, Gruen’s history is immensely rich and intertwined with American history. Unfortunately, the brand fell apart in the late 1950’s following the departure of the original Gruen family, scandal and financial disarray, which led to the selling of the name and closure of their Ohio offices. Under new ownership, Gruen continued producing watches until the mid-1970’s but was after poor management and financial instability took hold, the second wave of Gruen closed in 1976.
